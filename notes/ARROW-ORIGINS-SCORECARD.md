# Arrow origins scorecard

**Opened 2026-07-12.** Direct response to quant-phy #171 / Jon's equal-rigor
directive. No family gets a privileged standard, including the algebraic/HSMI
bet. The test is not "can this produce an arrow-looking monotone?" The test is:

> Where, if anywhere, does the family smuggle in the direction it claims to
> explain?

A family counts as genuine ignition only if its strongest version survives that
question.

## 1. Inherited boundary

**Strongest version.** The universe begins, or has a distinguished neck, in an
exceptionally ordered macrostate. In the Boltzmann/Past-Hypothesis idiom this
is low entropy. In Penrose's gravitational version, the initial singularity has
very low Weyl curvature, so gravitational entropy begins low. Carroll-Chen
`hep-th/0410270` tries to soften the special-beginning problem by using a
time-symmetric de Sitter / spontaneous-inflation setting with arrows emerging
away from a low-entropy neck.

**Strongest objection.** The family solves alignment, not origin. Once a
special boundary is granted, ordinary statistical mechanics explains why
entropy increases away from it. But the hard part is exactly why that boundary
is special. Inflation does not by itself remove the need for a low-entropy
condition; it can move the problem to the inflating patch, vacuum choice, or
measure on cosmological histories.

**Smuggled direction.** The arrow is smuggled in as the selected low-order
boundary itself. Even if the equations are time-reversal invariant, the
conditional question is asymmetric: "given this special end, what do typical
histories do away from it?"

**Tier.** Established as a conditional framework for thermodynamic-arrow
alignment; not established as an origin mechanism.

**Ignition verdict.** **No ignition.** This family inherits a direction from a
boundary condition. It remains a necessary control because any proposed
"ignition" that merely hides a Past Hypothesis here has failed.

## 2. Dynamical ignition

### 2.1 Janus / shape-complexity gravity

**Strongest version.** Barbour-Koslowski-Mercati `1310.5167`, `1409.0917`
argue that a closed, unconfined Newtonian gravitational system with vanishing
total energy and angular momentum generically has a Janus point of minimum
shape complexity. Complexity and record structure grow away from that point on
both branches. The arrow is not a chosen low-entropy initial macrostate; it is
a branch property of typical solutions in shape space.

**Strongest objection.** The phrase "generic" carries the load. The measure on
solutions, the restriction to the special closed model, and the mapping from
shape complexity to thermodynamic entropy must all survive scrutiny. A skeptic
can say the Janus point is a Past Hypothesis in relational clothing: a special
minimum is still doing the work, even if it is internal to the solution.

**Smuggled direction.** The most dangerous smuggling point is not the two-headed
solution itself; it is the branch selection by an internal observer and the use
of a monotone complexity parameter once a branch is chosen. If the branch
orientation is only "the side on which records increase," then records are
defining the arrow rather than deriving it.

**Tier.** Strong live candidate, but model-dependent.

**Ignition verdict.** **Best genuine-ignition candidate so far, not cleared.**
It deserves the same suspicion as HSMI: prove the Janus minimum is generic in a
measure not chosen to favor arrows, and prove records/thermodynamic arrows
really follow from shape complexity.

### 2.2 Causal-set sequential growth

**Strongest version.** Rideout-Sorkin `gr-qc/9904062` derives classical
sequential-growth dynamics from causality conditions, discrete general
covariance, and Bell causality. A causal set grows element by element; the
birth process looks like a microscopic "start" of temporal order.

**Strongest objection.** Causal-set label order is normally gauge under
discrete general covariance. If the order of birth labels is not physical, the
growth process may be a covariant way to generate a causet, not an ontic
flow. The physical object is the partial order, and partial order is not the
same as experienced becoming.

**Smuggled direction.** The Markov growth step starts from the empty causet and
adds new elements. If that sequential step is taken as physical, direction is
inserted in the dynamics. If it is gauge, the arrow has not been ignited yet.

**Tier.** Live but interpretively split.

**Ignition verdict.** **Hybrid.** The growth law is genuinely directional, but
the empty start and physical status of birth order are inherited inputs unless
the causal-set dynamics derives them.

### 2.3 Complexity second law

**Strongest version.** Brown-Susskind `1701.01107` argues for a second law of
quantum complexity: a system with less-than-maximal complexity overwhelmingly
evolves toward higher complexity, and uncomplexity is a resource. This is
especially compelling after thermal entropy saturates, as in black-hole
interior growth.

**Strongest objection.** It starts after uncomplexity exists. A generic state
in Hilbert space is already near maximal complexity. Without a derivation of
low initial complexity, the mechanism is a powerful arrow amplifier rather
than an origin story.

**Smuggled direction.** The smuggled object is the low-complexity initial
resource, plus the choice of forward circuit/time evolution with respect to a
simple Hamiltonian.

**Tier.** Strong dynamical arrow after an initial resource; incomplete as
origin.

**Ignition verdict.** **Hybrid.** Complexity growth is an excellent arrow
diagnostic and amplifier, but without a derived source of uncomplexity it
inherits its seed. It becomes genuine ignition only if that seed is supplied by
Janus typicality, an algebraic constraint, or some other non-boundary principle.

## 3. Observer / manufactured

**Strongest version.** The project's own P-B/S3/S4-arrow family is robust:
records, conditioning, tracing out, and algebra restriction create monotone
entropy or relative entropy for an embedded observer. The hardware record run
shows this operationally, and the GSL/crossed-product story is the
operator-algebraic high-energy version.

**Strongest objection.** This is not the start of time's flow. It explains why
an inside observer sees an arrow after some split into system, records, and
environment has already been made. Globally, the state or algebraic evolution
can remain reversible.

**Smuggled direction.** Direction enters through the initial product/low-entropy
environment, the choice to trace records after they are written, the future
orientation of decoherence, or the inclusion direction used in GSL-style
restriction. "Records form" is already a temporally oriented statement unless
the record-forming process is derived from a deeper mechanism.

**Tier.** Established operational arrow; not an origin mechanism.

**Ignition verdict.** **No origin.** This family is a necessary experimental and
phenomenological target, but it manufactures the arrow from an inside view. It
should not be allowed to masquerade as ignition.

## 4. Algebraic positive generator

**Strongest version.** Tomita-Takesaki theory gives state-dependent modular
flow. A half-sided modular inclusion gives a positive generator and the `ax+b`
relation by Borchers-Wiesbrock; Ceyhan-Faulkner `1812.04683` ties the relevant
positive generator to ANEC/QNEC in QFT. This is mathematically sharper than a
coarse-graining arrow: a standard inclusion produces a one-sided semigroup of
algebras.

**Strongest objection.** The input may already contain the direction. Thermal
time is state-dependent; choosing the vacuum/KMS state is not neutral. The QFT
spectrum condition `P >= 0` is usually an axiom or representation choice. A
half-sided inclusion specifies which side is half-sided. The algebraic theorem
may show that direction is rigid once these inputs are given, not how the
direction starts.

**Smuggled direction.** The likely smuggling points are:

1. **State choice:** the cyclic-separating vacuum/KMS state may already encode
   the time orientation.
2. **Spectrum condition:** positive energy is imposed by the representation
   rather than derived.
3. **Half-sidedness:** choosing `a >= 0` or `t <= 0` selects an orientation of
   inclusion.
4. **Null-cut orientation:** ANEC/QNEC applications choose a future null
   direction.
5. **Entropy bridge:** known QNEC/GSL readouts often pass through relative
   entropy, returning to the observer/manufactured family.

**Tier.** Rigorous conditional mathematics; origin status unproven.

**Ignition verdict.** **Candidate only if derived.** HSMI clears the scorecard
only if the standard inclusion and positive generator arise from a deeper
time-symmetric mechanism, or if it can be unified with Janus/complexity as the
local algebraic form of dynamical ignition.

## 5. Current ranking under the no-smuggling test

1. **Janus / shape-complexity dynamics** is the leading genuine-ignition
   candidate, but only if its typicality measure and record formation are not a
   Past Hypothesis in disguise.
2. **Algebraic HSMI / positive generator** is the sharpest local theorem, but
   it currently risks smuggling direction through state choice, spectrum
   condition, and half-sidedness.
3. **Causal-set growth** and **complexity second law** are hybrid: they contain
   real directed growth, but currently inherit an empty-start or uncomplexity
   seed.
5. **Inherited** and **observer/manufactured** families are essential controls
   but do not pass as origins.

## 6. Unifying test

The strongest possible synthesis remains:

> Janus/complexity supplies the global start; HSMI supplies the local algebraic
> normal form of each branch.

To test it, build or analyze a Janus branch with record algebras `M_a`. The
unification passes only if the branch parameter that orders increasing
complexity also orders a nested algebra net with a positive modular generator.
It fails if complexity grows without any extractable positive-inclusion
semigroup, or if HSMI succeeds as a standalone kinematic structure with no
record/complexity growth.

## 7. Reconciliation with quant-phy #172

**Finite-HSMI no-go.** Agreed, with precise scope. An **exact** half-sided
modular inclusion with nontrivial positive `ax+b` generator is not a finite
matrix phenomenon. In finite dimension the modular flow is inner/type-I, and
the trace obstruction `tr([D,P]) = 0` forbids an exact nonzero
`[D,P] = iP`. Finite/lattice constructions can be regulator or obstruction
demos, not literal HSMI ignition. Araki-Zsido `math/0412061` is the right
structure-theorem reference to keep next to Borchers/Wiesbrock.

**DSSYK/Krylov pivot.** Agreed as the honest buildable capstone, with one
caveat. Leutheusser-Liu `2112.12156` makes the type III_1/HSMI emergence
large-`N` and holographic; Gesteau `2310.13733` treats HSMI appearance as an
arrow-of-time diagnostic; DSSYK/Krylov work (`2510.13986`, plus spectral
complexity `2507.23667`) gives a Project-2-computable complexity arrow. That
does **not** prove exact finite HSMI. It tests the bridge:

> Does spectral/Krylov complexity growth in the DSSYK/de Sitter arena realize
> the same positive-order structure that HSMI captures in the large-`N`/type
> III_1 limit?

So the finite `ax+b` toy should be demoted to a no-go/regulator exhibit. The
capstone should pivot to DSSYK/Krylov spectral complexity as the buildable
Family-3-to-Family-6 bridge.

## 9. quant-phy amendments (from the verified origin/ignition sweep)

**A. Even Family 1 is trying to escape to ignition — strengthens the taxonomy.**
Beyond Carroll-Chen, two recent papers explicitly try to make the low-entropy
past *typical* rather than *postulated*: Arrighi-Dowek-Durbec `2306.07121` (a
reversible graph-rewriting toy where entropy rises as a structural side-effect,
no improbable initial state) and Lazarovici-Reichert `1809.04646` (for
unconfined/gravitating systems, *typical* initial conditions already give
two-sided growth — the explicit philosophical bridge Family 1 → Family 3). Also
Al-Khalili-Chen `2405.03418` sharpens the Past Hypothesis to a low-*entanglement*
initial state. Net: the field itself is migrating from INHERITED toward IGNITED,
which is exactly why Family 3 is where the action is.

**B. The unification observable is SPECTRAL — this sharpens §6/§7 and the capstone.**
Miyaji et al. `2507.23667` show the complexity arrow's linear-growth-then-
saturation follows from **RMT level repulsion** — i.e. the arrow is read off the
*spectrum* of the generator, not from a thermodynamic gradient. The modular
positive generator (Family 6) is itself a **spectrum condition** (P ≥ 0). So the
Family-3↔Family-6 bridge most plausibly routes through a *spectral* quantity, and
the concrete capstone test becomes:

> In DSSYK (our Phase-4/5 machinery), does the Krylov/spectral-complexity arrow
> and a modular-positivity diagnostic track the **same spectral quantity** (level
> statistics / sign of an effective generator)? If yes, complexity growth is the
> *observable* and modular positivity is the *origin* of one arrow. If the
> complexity arrow appears with no extractable positive-inclusion structure, the
> unification fails.

This is a **conjecture/proposal**, not a result — flagged as such. But it makes
§6's unifying test computable in the arena we already own (Project-2), which is
the honest form of "igniting a flow" given the finite-HSMI no-go.

**C. Two references for the smuggled-direction column + a niche.** Buchholz-
Fredenhagen `2305.11709` ("Arrow of time and quantum physics") is the explicit
"the spectrum condition + causal orientation is a *primitive* of local quantum
physics" position — i.e. it *concedes* Family 6's smuggling and argues the
orientation is irreducible; keep it next to the §4 objection as the strongest
statement of "P ≥ 0 is imposed, not derived." Separately: my sweep found **no
2020-2026 paper that systematically compares all six mechanisms** under one test.
This scorecard is arguably that missing survey — a possible outward-note niche
once the DSSYK bridge is tested.
