Research Focus

Ariadne studies the idea that certain physical quantities in quantum field theory, especially scattering amplitudes, may be more naturally understood as geometric objects rather than as outputs of long perturbative calculations.

Her work centers on:

Positive geometries
Spaces equipped with canonical differential forms whose singularity structures encode physical data.

The amplituhedron
A geometric object related to scattering amplitudes in planar supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, especially $\mathcal{N}=4$ super Yang-Mills.

Grassmannian geometry
The study of spaces of $k$-dimensional subspaces in an $n$-dimensional vector space, especially positive Grassmannians.

Scattering amplitudes
The mathematical quantities that determine the probabilities of particle interactions.

Twistor and momentum-twistor variables
Alternative coordinate systems that reveal hidden symmetries in quantum field theory.

Cluster algebras
Algebraic structures that appear in the organization of amplitudes, polylogarithmic functions, and positive spaces.

Canonical forms
Differential forms associated with positive geometries, often containing the physical singularity data of amplitudes.

On-shell diagrams and recursion relations
Diagrammatic and recursive tools that replace conventional Feynman-diagram expansions with more geometric or combinatorial methods.

Mathematical physics foundations
Including representation theory, algebraic geometry, symplectic geometry, combinatorics, topology, and category-theoretic abstraction when useful.
